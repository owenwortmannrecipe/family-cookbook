💡 Cooking Tips

Cooking Tips & Techniques

Essential knowledge every home cook should have.

Knife Skills

🔪

Keep your knife sharp

A sharp knife is safer than a dull one. Sharpen monthly and hone before each use.

🖐️

Use the claw grip

Curl your fingertips under when chopping, using knuckles as a guide for the blade.

Heat & Cooking

🍳

Preheat your pan

Always preheat before adding oil or food. A hot pan prevents sticking and creates better browning.

🌡️

Use a meat thermometer

Chicken: 165°F · Pork: 145°F · Beef (medium): 135°F · Fish: 145°F

⏱️

Let meat rest

Rest steaks and roasts 5–10 min after cooking. Juices redistribute throughout the meat.

Baking

⚖️

Weigh your ingredients

Baking is a science. Weighing flour is far more accurate than volume measures.

🥚

Room temperature ingredients

Pull butter and eggs out 30–60 minutes before baking for lighter, more even results.

Flavor Building

🧂

Season in layers

Add salt at each stage of cooking for deeper, more rounded flavor throughout.

🍋

Acid brightens everything

A squeeze of lemon or splash of vinegar at the end lifts and brightens any dish.

🌿 Seasonal Guide

Seasonal Cooking Guide

Cook with what's fresh — food tastes better and costs less in season.

🌸

Spring

Asparagus, peas, artichokes, spinach, radishes, strawberries, rhubarb, spring onions.

☀️

Summer

Tomatoes, zucchini, corn, peppers, eggplant, peaches, berries, watermelon, basil.

🍂

Fall

Pumpkin, squash, apples, pears, Brussels sprouts, sweet potatoes, cranberries, kale.

❄️

Winter

Root vegetables, citrus, cabbage, leeks, pomegranate, broccoli, cauliflower, endive.

Idaho Seasonal Highlights

Living in Boise, Idaho means access to incredible local produce. Idaho is famous for its potatoes year-round. Treasure Valley farmers markets run May–October with exceptional stone fruits, sweet corn, and peppers in summer. Fall brings local apple orchards with dozens of varieties not found in stores.

Preserving the Harvest

🫙

Canning

Water-bath canning for high-acid foods (tomatoes, fruit, pickles). Pressure canner for low-acid vegetables.

🧊

Freezing

Blanch vegetables before freezing. Most vegetables last 8–12 months frozen.
